Research Process

Implementation. Research process: Two groups listen to the company's audio assets. One group has heard these assets before, while the other hears them for the first time. After listening, both groups answer the client's questions. Based on their responses, a report is generated for the client. Initially, CS managers handled the entire process. They surveyed clients via email, filled out questionnaires, sent data to panellists providers, compiled presentations, and presented to clients. These are my primary sources of information.

LitRes Libraries

Overview

Litres not only sells books, but it also provides a library service. The digital library works just like a physical public library. There is a book fund, which is filled with budget money. Employees and ordinary readers can purchase books to add to the fund through the LitRes e-store. The administrators configure the rights to add books to the collection.

Libraries rely on government funding, which is provided every six months for their budgets. Additionally, libraries fill their book funds with help from their users.

Problem

The library spent the entire budget for the quarter within the first few weeks, which posed a problem that needed to be solved with a graphical interface.

The second problem is that library staff are mostly elderly people who do not want to mess around with computers and programs, so we need to motivate them, to make them think of the value of this work.

Hypothesis

By default, every new reader has the right to buy books for the fund. These rights are the same for all libraries, and not everyone is aware of their existence. As a result, both rich and poor libraries have the same approach to using budget money. To address this issue, we must first individualize the rights to replenish the fund.

Solution

I struggled to find a way to effectively motivate people to pay attention to the individual rights settings of new users. One day, while working at the Fyodor Dostoyevsky Library, I observed the process of issuing electronic library cards. In Russian culture, there is a strong appreciation for power, regardless of its magnitude. Being in a position of authority is seen as a privilege. I decided to leverage this cultural trait to my advantage.

I empowered the librarian to control the future of individuals, creating a sense of accountability. This influence extended to the staff, who began editing the rights of existing users. This move saved the budget considerably and improved the quality of the library's book collection.

LitRes Reader

Overview

Any user can upload a book from their computer to LitRes, where it will be added to their list of purchased books. Users can then download the book in any format to their computer or read it using the built-in reader, which is synchronized across all devices. If available, users can also access an audiobook from the point where they stopped reading.

Problem

The reader has been designed to be as flexible as possible. Users can customize fonts, background colors, text, height, and line length. However, users may unknowingly ruin their experience by making inappropriate adjustments.

Research

After conducting a study, it was found that people unknowingly compromise their user experience by using non-contrasting background colors and fonts that are too small when the book is wide. Typography is a whole science that cannot be ignored.

Solution

To address this issue, we developed an algorithm that compensates for the baseline and format of the book depending on the reader's chosen scale. We also picked safe colors to ensure sufficient contrast and prevent eye fatigue, while limiting the reader's freedom.

The lesson we learned is that you are not your users, and it is essential to use more than two analytics systems to verify hypotheses.
